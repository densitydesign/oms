Compared to Birth Control, Family planning article has a shorter and simpler evolution.

The article was created in 2003, pointing to ‘Birth Control’ article. The first edit come in 2006, but we have no article structure until august in the same year. Article had a troubled beginning, since added sections were quickly removed.

From August 2006 article had an organic growth, and didn’t require any revision of the structure. Its evolution is mainly based on the development of sub-section particularly focused on the national policies on the topic.

U.S. Policies were the first added. It is not surprising: even if it is seen as the ‘global’ version fo the encyclopedia, en.wikipedia reflect mainly U.S. point of view. In the same month China one-child policy was added.

In following years were added Iran, India, Hong Kong, Pakistan, the Philippines, Ireland, Singapore, United Kingdom and Russia.

It is interesting to see that in December 2012 the section “assisted reproductive technology” was added, to cover not only the prevention of pregnancies through birth control methods but also the technical solution to support it.
