The ‘Birth control’ article has a long history, back when Wikipedia was unknown to the large public. We lost trace of the first editor, as the article was created with the very first version of the software, then replaced by the actual one in January 2002. Article begins with conversionBot migrating the article on the freshly-coded MediaWiki platform.

For the first two years the article remained unmodified, if we don’t count an edit in 2003 just removing semicolons.

Looking at his evolution, we can see that structure evolved through ‘thematic’ time spans: first defining birth control methods, then its history and the related ethical issues.

While growing, article needed some major revision of the structure, in particular defining the order of the topic. One clear example is the switch between history and methods description in 2011: as the historic section become too big, users understood that methods description was the main core of the article, and therefore the first element to present in the article.

On the visualization is possible to follow the evolution of each section. Particularly interesting is the section dedicated to ethical issues whose subject moved, toward years, from the religious to a more national and then social aspects.

The chapter, at beginning, was called ‘Religious and cultural attitude to birth control’. The first structural edit was aimed to explain the protestant and catholic view on it. Judaism and Islam were then added.
