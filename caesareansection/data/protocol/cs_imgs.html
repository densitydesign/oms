harvesting the image corpus

We queried Google.com, Google.fr, Google.co.uk, Google.it (in incognito mode) for the following queries.

  • .COM: Cesarean, "Cesarean delivery"
  • .CO.UK: Caesarean, "Caesarean delivery"
  • .FR: Césarienne, "Accouchement par césarienne"
  • .IT: Cesareo, "Taglio cesareo"
  • "Cesarean delivery"
  • "Caesarean delivery"
  • "Operative delivery"
  • Caesarean Cesarean

For each query we collected the first 1000 images.

selecting and tagging the images

We qualified and tagged all the images accordingly to their contents and excluding from the corpus images not concerned with CS (i.e. cesarean section on animals, images of Porto Cesareo a well known seaside town in south Italy). Each image can belong to more than one group (e.g. a photo of nurse helping during a childbirth will belong to “HC PROVIDERS;BODY; and CHILDBIRTH” categories).
The following categories were used:

  • Body
  • Data
  • Delivery
  • Exams
  • Family
  • HC Providers
  • Hospital
  • Illustration
  • Men
  • New Borns
  • Nursery
  • Picture
  • Publication
  • Room
  • Tools
  • VIP
  • Others

Finally we computed the dominant color of each image.

color extraction and rich prospect browsing

The images have been organized by language and by category through an elastic list. The visualization allows to observe the quantity of images extracted for each language and each category, by dynamically reorganizing the lists after a selection. By selecting a specific category it is possible to switch between pictures and dominant colors (sorted according to the page rank algorithm) to see the composition of each category.
