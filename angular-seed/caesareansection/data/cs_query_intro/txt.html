Harvesting the Relevant Webpages

We designed 9 different queries to collect the most relevant web-pages addressing the Cesarean Section. The different queries have been designed to address as much as possible “nuances” of the Cesarean Section issues: from the more medical ones to the more daily ones.
We qualified and tagged all the webpages accordingly to their contents obtaining three main webpages groups:

medical information

These pages contain medical explanations and theories proposed with neutral tone. Often, they are stating medical or historical facts or physiotherapy and recovery-related issues.

contentious information

These pages are fostering the real debate and are dealing with issues related to the adoption rates, humanization of birth and humanization of caesarean section.

experiential information

These pages contents related to experience from women, fathers as well experience or opinions from VIPs and celebrities.

NOTE
If medical facts refer to a controversy or if they are stated within a context of controversy or pointing to a controversy, they were classified as medical and contentious.
In medical articles that present evidence that is conflicting based on whatever parameter (quality of evidence, conflicting results or lack of studies) we will tag as medical and contentious.
For pages in which women (women’s experiences) discuss controversial issues, these were classified as both experiential and contentious.
In controversies that refer to the past (for example in historical articles), these will be tagged as medical and not contentious because we are only interested in current issues.
