tagging notes

  1. M refers to pages in which medical facts, explanations and theories may be explained without posing a mess or with high emotional tone. It would be rather a neutral tone.
  2. If medical facts refer to a controversy or if they are stated within a context of controversy or pointing to a controversy, they were classified as M and C.
  3. Pages dealing with issues related to humanization of birth, humanization of caesarean section were considered and classified under controversies.
  4. For initial pages of sites where no much information is shown, we did enter to assess more valuable information if it was a matter of one click; however, for pages in which several other pages could be opened, these were not opened and only assessment of the first page was conducted.
  5. If the link is an opening page with nothing that can be classified, it is tagged as NI.
  6. When in doubt, we used expert judgment so a certain level of subjectivity is always present.
  7. For pages in which women (women experiences) discuss controversial issues, these were classified as both E and C.
  8. In articles that present an Experience, plus a Controversy and Medical facts, then should tag as E, C and M. This applies to all pages, including opening/home pages.
  9. In medical articles that present evidence that is conflicting based on whatever parameter (quality of evidence, conflicting results or lack of studies) we will tag as C and M.
  10. In Controversies that refer to the past (for example in historical articles), these will be tagged as M and not C because we are only interested in current Controversies (only these, will be tagged as C).
